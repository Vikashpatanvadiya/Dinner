# PRD: Live Restaurant Queue & Pre-Order Platform

## 1. Problem Statement

Customers visiting busy restaurants during peak hours face long, unpredictable waits (30-60 minutes) because:
- They have no way to see how many people are waiting at a restaurant before they arrive.
- Restaurant staff give vague verbal estimates ("15-20 minutes") that are often wrong.
- Customers can't join a queue remotely — they have to physically stand outside to get a number.
- Walking to a different restaurant is a gamble — it might have the same or worse wait.
- Even after joining a queue, customers can't pre-order food, so cooking time is wasted *after* they're seated instead of overlapping with their wait time.

**Goal:** Build one web app with two sides:
- **Customer side** (no login) — scan a QR code or browse the platform to see real-time queue status at restaurants, join a queue remotely, track their live position, and optionally pre-order food while waiting.
- **Restaurant side** (requires sign up/login) — a dashboard where restaurant staff manage the live queue, see incoming pre-orders, and call the next customer.

Both sides live in **one single web app / one Replit project / one database**, so queue updates are instant on both ends without needing to sync two separate apps.

## 3. Customer Side — Features (No Login)

### 3.1 Entry Points
1. **QR Code Scan** — Each restaurant has a unique QR code (generated on their dashboard) displayed at their entrance. Scanning it opens that restaurant's live queue page directly.
2. **Platform Discovery Page** — Customers can open the app directly (without scanning) and see a list of nearby/all registered restaurants with their current live wait status, so they can compare before choosing where to go.

### 3.2 Discovery Page (Browse Restaurants)
- List/grid of all restaurants registered on the platform showing:
  - Restaurant name, cuisine type, photo
  - **Current queue length** (e.g., "8 people waiting")
  - **Estimated wait time** (auto-calculated, see Section 6)
  - Status badge: 🟢 No wait / 🟡 Moderate wait / 🔴 Long wait
- Sort/filter by: shortest wait time, distance (if location permission given), cuisine type
- Tapping a restaurant opens its individual **Restaurant Queue Page**

### 3.3 Restaurant Queue Page (after QR scan or selecting from discovery)
- Restaurant name, address, photo, current live queue count, estimated wait time
- **"Join Queue" button** — opens a simple form:
  - Name
  - Phone number
  - Party size (number of people)
- On submit: customer instantly receives:
  - Their **queue number** (e.g., #14)
  - **Position in line** (e.g., "5 people ahead of you")
  - **Live estimated wait time**, auto-updating
- This queue ticket is saved to the customer's browser session (localStorage equivalent — see technical notes) so if they refresh or reopen the page/link, they see their live status again without re-entering details.

### 3.4 Live Queue Tracking Screen
- Shows in real time (auto-refreshing, e.g., every 5-10 seconds or via live updates):
  - Customer's current position (e.g., "You are #14 — 5 people ahead of you")
  - Updated estimated wait time
  - A simple progress indicator (e.g., a visual queue bar or countdown style UI)
- **Pre-Order while waiting:**
  - When the customer is within a configurable threshold (e.g., "3 people or fewer ahead of you" — restaurant can set this number), a **"Pre-Order Now"** button becomes active/highlighted.
  - Customer browses a simple menu (set by the restaurant) → adds items to cart → places order.
  - Order status shown: Placed → Being Prepared → Ready
  - The idea: food prep starts while they're still walking in/finishing their wait, so the food is ready closer to when they're seated.
- Customer can also **leave the queue** anytime (cancel button), which removes them and shifts everyone else up.

### 3.5 Bill View (Simple, Phase 1)
- After being seated/served, customer can view their order summary and total bill on the same page/link (no login needed — tied to their queue session).
- Payment: show **"Pay at Counter"** as default, with an optional **"Pay Online"** button (UPI/card placeholder integration — can be mocked first, real gateway added later).

## 4. Restaurant Side — Features (Login Required)

### 4.1 Authentication
- **Sign Up:** Restaurant name, owner name, email, phone, password, address, cuisine type
- **Login:** Email + password
- Each restaurant gets a unique dashboard and a unique QR code tied to their account (auto-generated on signup, downloadable/printable as PNG/PDF)

### 4.2 Dashboard — Live Queue Management
- Real-time list of all customers currently in queue, showing:
  - Queue number, name, phone number, party size, time joined, wait duration so far
- Actions per customer:
  - **"Call Next"** — marks the top customer as called/notified, moves them to a "Called" state
  - **"Seat"** — marks customer as seated, removes them from active queue
  - **"Remove/No-show"** — removes a customer who didn't show up
- Queue automatically re-numbers/shifts when someone is removed or seated
- Restaurant can **set/update estimated average wait time per table** (used in wait time calculations)
- Restaurant can pause new queue joins (e.g., "Queue Closed" toggle) when fully booked or closing soon

### 4.3 Pre-Order Management
- View incoming pre-orders from customers currently in queue
- Update order status: Placed → Preparing → Ready
- Simple menu management screen: add/edit/remove menu items with name, price, category, availability toggle

### 4.4 Bill Management (Phase 1 - Simple)
- View generated bill per customer/table (auto-built from their pre-order items; manual add-on option for items ordered after being seated)
- Mark bill as "Paid" (cash/counter) or view if customer paid online
- Basic daily summary: total customers served, total orders, total revenue (simple dashboard stats)

### 4.5 QR Code Management
- View/download/print their unique QR code
- QR code links directly to their **Restaurant Queue Page** (Section 3.3)

## 5. Data Model (Suggested)

```
Restaurant
- id, name, email, password_hash, phone, address, cuisine_type, 
  qr_code_url, avg_wait_per_table_mins, queue_open (boolean), created_at

QueueEntry
- id, restaurant_id, queue_number, customer_name, customer_phone, 
  party_size, status (waiting | called | seated | removed), 
  joined_at, session_token (used to let customer re-access their ticket)

MenuItem
- id, restaurant_id, name, price, category, is_available

Order
- id, queue_entry_id, restaurant_id, items (list of {menu_item_id, qty, price}), 
  status (placed | preparing | ready | served), total_amount, 
  payment_status (pending | paid_online | paid_cash), created_at
```

## 6. Wait Time Calculation Logic (Simple Version for V1)

```
estimated_wait_time_for_new_customer = 
    (number_of_people_ahead_in_queue / avg_party_seated_per_turn) 
    × restaurant's avg_wait_per_table_mins
```
- Keep it simple for V1: `estimated_wait = people_ahead × avg_wait_per_table_mins`
- Recalculate live every time the queue changes (someone seated/removed/joins)
- This can be refined later with real historical data, but for V1 a simple linear estimate is enough to be useful.

## 7. Real-Time Updates — Technical Approach

To make the queue feel "live" without a login system on the customer side:
- Use **polling** (simplest for V1 on Replit): customer's browser fetches updated queue position every 5-10 seconds.
- (Stretch goal, if Replit + chosen stack supports it easily) Use **WebSockets** for instant push updates instead of polling.
- Customer's queue ticket/session should be tracked via a **unique session token stored in the URL or localStorage**, NOT a login — e.g., after joining, redirect to `/queue/{restaurant_id}/{session_token}` so refreshing or sharing that link still shows their live ticket.

---

## 8. Suggested Tech Stack (for Replit Agent)

- **Frontend:** React (Vite) with Tailwind CSS — clean, mobile-first design since most customers will use this on their phones
- **Backend:** Node.js + Express (or Replit's recommended full-stack template)
- **Database:** Replit's built-in database (Replit DB) or PostgreSQL (via Replit's PostgreSQL integration) — use PostgreSQL if available, since relational data (restaurants → queue entries → orders) fits better than key-value
- **Auth (Restaurant side only):** Simple email/password auth with hashed passwords (bcrypt); session-based or JWT
- **QR Code Generation:** Use a library like `qrcode` (npm) to generate QR codes pointing to each restaurant's queue page URL
- **Real-time updates:** Start with polling (`setInterval` fetch every 5-10s) for V1 simplicity; mention WebSockets (Socket.io) as a stretch goal if time allows

## 9. Page/Route Map

**Customer-facing (public, no login):**
- `/` — Discovery page (list of all restaurants + live wait status)
- `/restaurant/:id` — Restaurant queue page (join queue form + current status)
- `/queue/:id/:session_token` — Customer's live queue tracking + pre-order + bill page

**Restaurant-facing (auth required):**
- `/restaurant/signup` — Sign up form
- `/restaurant/login` — Login form
- `/dashboard` — Live queue management (default after login)
- `/dashboard/menu` — Menu management
- `/dashboard/orders` — Pre-order management
- `/dashboard/qr` — View/download QR code
- `/dashboard/stats` — Simple daily summary

## 10. V1 Scope (Build This First)

✅ Restaurant sign up/login
✅ Restaurant dashboard: live queue list, call next, seat, remove customer
✅ QR code generation per restaurant
✅ Customer: join queue via QR or discovery page (name, phone, party size — no login)
✅ Customer: live queue position + estimated wait time (polling-based updates)
✅ Customer: pre-order food when near the front of the queue
✅ Restaurant: menu management + view/update pre-order status
✅ Simple bill view (pay at counter default; online payment can be a placeholder button for now)

### Phase 2 (Later)
- Real online payment gateway integration (Razorpay/Stripe)
- WebSocket-based instant updates instead of polling
- Location-based sorting on discovery page (using browser geolocation)
- Analytics for restaurant (peak hours, average wait trends)
- SMS/WhatsApp notifications when customer's turn is near

## 11. Design Notes

- Mobile-first — most customers will be on their phones while standing outside a restaurant
- Customer flow should be **extremely fast** — joining a queue should take under 30 seconds (just name, phone, party size, tap submit)
- Make the live queue number large and prominent — this is the core trust-building element of the app
- Restaurant dashboard should be usable on a tablet at the host stand, with large tap targets for "Call Next" / "Seat" / "Remove" actions
